Policy Analysis Published: Summer 2025

To What Extent Can U.S. and Caribbean-Led Interventions Stabilize Haiti While Ensuring Its Long-Term Sovereignty and Development?

Kush K. Dave

This comprehensive policy analysis examines Haiti's ongoing crisis through economic, geopolitical, security, and social lenses. The study evaluates the historical impact of foreign involvement in Haiti, analyzing both successes and failures to determine whether a more sustainable, sovereignty-centered approach is possible. The research explores alternative regional approaches that empower Haiti to establish resilient institutions, strengthen its economy, and reduce reliance on external assistance, while addressing the current crisis involving gang control over 80% of Port-au-Prince.

Keywords:
Haiti Crisis Foreign Intervention Caribbean Policy Sovereignty Development Security Reform
