Earthquake in Myanmar: An Analysis of the Situation

Devastating Impact on Infrastructure and Communities

Jackson Dorris
April 20, 2025
5 min read
320 views

Jackson Dorris

Journalism Director at GVMUN

jackson.dorris@gvmun.org

On March 28, 2025, at around 12:50 pm local time, an earthquake with a 7.7 magnitude was recorded in Myanmar. The devastation has been immense, with thousands of casualties and widespread infrastructure damage affecting communities across the country.

7.7 Magnitude Scale
3,500+ Casualties
65,000 Homes Destroyed
2.5M Tons of Debris

Key Information

The earthquake was so violent that it could be felt in Bangkok, Thailand. On April 17, 2025 at 2:32 am, another earthquake shook Myanmar 10 miles from Mandalay. The quake was measured to be 4.5 magnitude, and the destruction is devastating.

Geological Cause: Experts say that the quake was caused by the Sagaing fault, as it runs north to south straight through Myanmar. Continuous minor aftershocks have continued to be felt, slowing response times and affecting citizens and residents in many ways.

Implications Felt by Citizens

There have been thousands of casualties due to the earthquakes, with tallies continuing to climb as more damage and rubble are being cleared. Furthermore, the destruction of buildings, especially religious ones, has been devastating, with upwards of 8,400 monasteries, pagodas, and nunneries reduced to rubble.

Infrastructure Damage

Over 170 bridges, 203 parts of the main highway, and 580 dams were damaged or destroyed.

Educational Impact

2,500 schools have been destroyed, affecting thousands of children's access to education.
